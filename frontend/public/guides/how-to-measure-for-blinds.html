How to Measure for Window Blinds & Shades

5 min read  |  Measuring Guide

Accurate measurements are the foundation of perfectly fitting window shades. Whether you're ordering roller shades, zebra blinds, or any other window treatment, taking precise measurements ensures your shades look great and function properly. This guide walks you through the process step by step.

What You'll Need

  • Steel tape measure - Cloth tapes can stretch; steel is more accurate
  • Pencil and paper - To record your measurements
  • Step stool - For reaching the top of tall windows safely

Inside Mount vs Outside Mount

Before measuring, you need to decide how you want your shades mounted. This decision affects how you measure and the final look of your window treatment.

Inside Mount Outside Mount
Mounts within the window frame Mounts on the wall above/around the frame
Clean, built-in look Makes windows appear larger
Requires minimum 1.5" depth Works with any window depth
Some light leaks around edges Better light blocking coverage

Pro Tip

For blackout shades where maximum light blocking is important, outside mount is usually the better choice. It allows the shade to overlap the window frame, minimizing light gaps.

How to Measure for Inside Mount

Inside mount shades fit within your window frame for a clean, built-in appearance.

1

Measure the Width

Measure the width of the window opening at three points: top, middle, and bottom. Record the narrowest measurement. This ensures your shade will fit even if the window isn't perfectly square.

2

Measure the Height

Measure the height of the window opening at three points: left side, center, and right side. Record the longest measurement. This ensures full coverage.

3

Check the Depth

Measure the depth of your window frame from the front edge to the glass. For inside mount, you need at least 1.5 inches of depth for roller shades and 2.5 inches for zebra shades.

Important

For inside mount, provide the exact opening measurements. We will make the necessary deductions (typically 1/4" to 3/8") to ensure proper fit and operation.

How to Measure for Outside Mount

Outside mount shades attach to the wall or window casing above and around the window frame.

1

Measure the Width

Measure the area you want to cover, including any overlap beyond the window frame. We recommend adding 2-3 inches on each side of the window for better light blocking and a more substantial look.

2

Measure the Height

Measure from where you want the shade to start (usually 2-3 inches above the frame) to where you want it to end (usually at the window sill or below). Add the overlap to your measurement.

3

Check for Obstructions

Look for handles, locks, or other hardware that might interfere with the shade operation. Ensure you have at least 1 inch of clearance from any obstructions.

Recording Your Measurements

Always record measurements in the format: Width x Height (e.g., 36" x 48"). Measure to the nearest 1/8 inch for the most accurate fit.

Common Measurement Mistakes to Avoid

  • Rounding measurements - Always measure to the nearest 1/8 inch
  • Using a cloth tape - Cloth tapes stretch and give inaccurate readings
  • Measuring only once - Measure at least twice to confirm accuracy
  • Assuming windows are square - Most windows have slight variations
  • Forgetting depth for inside mount - Insufficient depth prevents proper installation

Measuring Multiple Windows

If you're ordering shades for multiple windows, even windows that look identical should be measured individually. Manufacturing tolerances mean that "matching" windows often have slight size differences.

Label each measurement clearly with the room and window location (e.g., "Living Room - Left Window: 36" x 48").
