Blackout Shades: Everything You Need to Know

6 min read  |  Room Darkening Guide

Whether you're a light sleeper, work night shifts, or just want to create a home theater experience, blackout shades are the solution for controlling light in any room. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about blackout window treatments - from understanding light blocking levels to installation tips for maximum darkness.

Understanding Light Blocking Levels

Not all "blackout" shades are created equal. The term is often used loosely in the industry. Here's how to understand what you're actually getting:

99%+
True Blackout
  • Light Filtering (25-50%) - Softens light, provides daytime privacy, but room stays bright
  • Room Darkening (70-90%) - Significantly reduces light, good for napping, but not complete darkness
  • Blackout (95-99%) - Blocks nearly all light, suitable for most bedrooms
  • True Blackout (99%+) - Maximum light blocking, ideal for night shift workers and home theaters

Pro Tip

Even true blackout fabric won't create complete darkness if light leaks around the edges. For maximum darkness, consider outside mount installation with overlap, or add side channels.

Types of Blackout Shades

Blackout Roller Shades

The most popular choice for bedrooms. A single layer of tightly woven or coated fabric rolls up into a compact cylinder. Clean, modern look that works with any decor. Our blackout roller shades use a white backing that reflects light and heat.

Blackout Zebra Shades

Combines the adjustable light control of zebra shades with blackout fabric. When the solid stripes align, you get complete darkness. When offset, soft filtered light enters. Perfect for spaces where you want versatility.

Blackout Cellular Shades

Honeycomb-structured shades that trap air for excellent insulation. The blackout versions add a foil or opaque layer inside the cells. Best for energy efficiency and sound absorption.

How to Achieve Maximum Darkness

Blackout Installation Checklist

  • Choose outside mount for better light blocking
  • Add 2-3 inches overlap on each side of the window
  • Extend above the window frame by 3-4 inches
  • Consider side channels to block edge gaps
  • Use a valance or fascia to cover the top gap
  • Check for light leaks around hardware

Inside Mount vs Outside Mount for Blackout

While inside mount looks cleaner, it inevitably leaves small gaps around the edges where light can seep in. For true darkness, outside mount is almost always the better choice.

With outside mount, you can:

  • Overlap the window frame to block edge light
  • Install higher to eliminate top gaps
  • Use wider shades to cover wall area
  • Add light-blocking accessories more easily

Common Mistake

Ordering blackout shades the exact size of the window opening. Always add overlap for outside mount, or accept that inside mount will have some light leakage around the edges.

Blackout Fabrics Explained

White-Backed Blackout

The fabric has a white backing that faces the window. This reflects sunlight and heat, keeping rooms cooler. The front can be any color. Most popular choice for bedrooms.

Foam-Backed Blackout

A layer of foam laminated to the fabric provides excellent light blocking and some noise reduction. Slightly thicker than standard blackout, so may have a larger roll diameter.

Double-Layer Blackout

Two layers of fabric with a blackout layer sandwiched between. Provides the best light blocking but at a higher cost. Often found in premium hotel installations.

Blackout Shades for Texas Homes

In Texas, blackout shades serve double duty - they block light AND reduce heat. Our summers are brutal, and windows are a major source of heat gain. Blackout shades with reflective backing can reduce solar heat gain by up to 45%, significantly lowering your AC bills.

For south and west-facing windows that get intense afternoon sun, blackout shades are particularly valuable. Even if you don't need darkness for sleeping, the energy savings alone make them worthwhile.

Motorized Blackout Options

Motorized blackout shades are increasingly popular, especially for bedrooms. Wake up naturally by scheduling your shades to open with the sunrise, or close them automatically at bedtime. Voice control through Alexa or Google Home makes operation effortless.

For hard-to-reach windows, motorized blackout shades eliminate the need for cords or manual adjustment while maintaining complete light control.
