Versatile Light Control with Signature Style

Blackout zebra shades represent the pinnacle of window shade innovation, combining the beautiful alternating stripe pattern of zebra shades with the complete light-blocking capability of blackout fabric. The result is a shade that adapts to your needs throughout the day.

During the day, offset the stripes to allow soft, filtered light into your room while maintaining privacy. At night or when you need complete darkness, simply align the opaque stripes to block 99%+ of incoming light. This versatility makes blackout zebra shades perfect for bedrooms, nurseries, home theaters, and any space where you want both style and light control.

Unlike traditional blackout roller shades that only offer two positions (up or down), blackout zebra shades let you fine-tune your light levels at any shade position. You can darken a room without fully closing your shades, maintaining a connection to the outside while controlling glare and light.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are blackout zebra shades?
Blackout zebra shades combine the elegant alternating stripe design of traditional zebra shades with opaque blackout fabric. This gives you the versatility of zebra shades plus complete room darkening when the stripes are aligned.
How dark do blackout zebra shades make a room?
When the opaque stripes are fully aligned, blackout zebra shades block 99%+ of light, similar to traditional blackout roller shades. When stripes are offset, soft filtered light enters through the sheer bands.
Are blackout zebra shades good for bedrooms?
Yes! Blackout zebra shades are ideal for bedrooms. You get complete darkness for sleeping, with the option to let in filtered light during the day without raising the shade.
How do I operate blackout zebra shades?
Pull the shade down to lower it to any position. Then use the adjustment cord or pull the bottom rail slightly to shift the front layer, aligning or offsetting the stripes to control light levels.
What colors are available?
Our blackout zebra shades come in a range of neutral colors including white, ivory, gray, charcoal, and more. These versatile tones complement any interior design style.
