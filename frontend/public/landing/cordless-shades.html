Safety First, Style Always

Corded blinds have been identified as a serious hazard for young children and pets. The CPSC reports that window cord strangulation is a leading cause of injury in homes with small children. Our cordless shades eliminate this risk entirely, giving you peace of mind while your little ones explore.

But safety doesn't mean sacrificing style. Our cordless shades come in the same beautiful fabrics, colors, and styles as our corded options. From light-filtering to blackout, roller to zebra - every shade type is available in a cordless version.

Operating cordless shades is simple and intuitive. Just grasp the bottom rail and gently pull down to lower the shade, or give a slight tug upward to raise it. The internal spring mechanism holds the shade at any position, so you can set it exactly where you want it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are cordless shades safe for children?
Yes! Cordless shades eliminate the strangulation hazard posed by traditional corded blinds. They meet all CPSC child safety standards and are recommended for homes with young children and pets.
How do cordless shades work?
Cordless shades use a spring mechanism inside the roller tube. Simply pull the bottom of the shade down to lower it, or give a gentle tug up to raise it. The shade stays in any position you set it.
Are cordless shades more expensive?
Cordless shades typically cost $10-20 more than corded versions due to the spring mechanism. Many parents find this small investment worthwhile for the peace of mind and safety benefits.
Can cordless shades be hard to operate?
Not at all! Our cordless shades are designed for smooth, effortless operation. The spring tension is calibrated for easy raising and lowering at any position.
